Literature Survey

Sri Lanka is the 4th largest coconut exporter in the world

Snakebite envenomation remains a serious public health concern, particularly in tropical countries like Sri Lanka. Traditional education methods often fail to prepare people for emergencies. VR-based education offers a dynamic and safe solution that improves retention and decision-making in high-stress scenarios.

The use of AI and IoT in VR platforms enhances realism, providing features such as venom flow simulations, species recognition, and personalized learning. This interactive approach bridges the gap between theory and practical knowledge, empowering users to respond effectively to snakebite emergencies.

Research Problem

How can an immersive VR platform train users effectively in snake identification, safe handling, and first-aid response in high-risk areas?

Proposed Solution

SerpentSphere is a VR platform providing safe, interactive training on snakebite-related scenarios using advanced VR, AI, and IoT technologies.

Research Objectives

  • Train safe snake handling using tools.
  • Enable accurate snake identification and venom extraction practice.
  • Visualize venom effects and teach proper first-aid response.
  • Use AI for adaptive, real-time learning.

Methodology

SerpentSphere uses a user-centered design with three core VR modules:

  1. Snake Handling: Practice tool-based snake handling in simulated forests.
  2. Identification & Venom Extraction: Learn with detailed 3D snake models and interactive instruments.
  3. Venom Effects & First Aid: Visualize venom effects and get guided first-aid training.
